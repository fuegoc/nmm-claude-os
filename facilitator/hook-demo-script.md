# Hook Demo Script

The 2-minute demo you run at 0:03 of the session to sell the premise. This is the whole reason anyone pays attention for the next 55 minutes. If the hook lands, everything else is easy. If it flops, you are climbing uphill.

## The setup

Screen-share your Claude Desktop with two chats visible side by side. Left: a fresh, empty chat outside any project. Right: a chat inside your personal Claude OS Project with the Notion connector live.

Both chats get the same prompt. The contrast does the work.

## The prompt

Pick a real student question from a recent Discord post. Not a fabricated one. The realism is what makes it land. Something that matches this shape:

> "Hey, I'm stuck on module 2. I don't really understand what a pattern interrupt is. Can you explain and maybe show me an example?"

## Left chat (cold, no project)

Paste the prompt. Claude gives a generic answer about pattern interrupts with no voice, no module reference, no FAQ link, probably 200+ words, probably with the word "Certainly!" somewhere, probably with an em dash. Let it run to completion. Leave the tab visible.

## Right chat (inside your Role Project, Notion FAQ connected)

Paste the exact same prompt. Claude queries the FAQ via Notion, finds the relevant entry, drafts a reply in your voice (short, no filler, points to the actual module 2 resource), and returns it cleanly.

Note: you do NOT need the Student Reply skill installed for this demo. Your Role Project + Notion connector alone are enough to make the contrast. The skill makes it even better, but the demo lands with just the Project + connector.

## What to say during the demo

Keep it to three lines, no more:

1. **Before running the left chat:** *"Here is how you have been using Claude."*
2. **Before running the right chat:** *"Here is how you will be using it by the end of this hour."*
3. **After both finish:** *"Left took me 20 seconds and I cannot send it. Right took 20 seconds and I can send it with 2 small edits. That difference, compounded across 40 student replies a day, is why we are here."*

Stop talking. Move to the 3-shelf model.

## What to NOT say

- Do not explain how the right chat worked. That is what the next 55 minutes are for.
- Do not apologize for the left chat's output. It is generic on purpose. That is the point.
- Do not compare to ChatGPT or other tools. Irrelevant.
- Do not hype. The contrast sells itself.

## Fallback if the demo breaks

If your Notion connector is down live, the demo still works with reduced impact. Just paste the prompt into your Role Project chat and let Claude answer from the Project Instructions alone (without FAQ lookup). The voice match will still be obviously better than the cold chat, even without the FAQ link.

If both chats break entirely, open a recording you made during rehearsal. Which is why step 1 of pre-flight is "do a dry run."

# Wins Log

One line at the end of every workday. That is the whole ritual.

The Wins Log is the difference between treating Claude as a tool you forget about and treating it as a system that gets sharper every week. Without this log, your Role Project stays stale and your skill library plateaus. With it, both compound.

---

## The rule

At the end of every workday, add one line with three parts:

```
[DATE] — Used [skill name] for [task]. What worked / what I'd tweak.
```

That is it. Thirty seconds. No paragraphs. If you want to write more, do it on a separate day as a longer reflection, but the daily line is non-negotiable.

---

## Examples

```
2026-04-14 — Used Morning Brief first thing. Caught 2 student threads I would have missed. Next time, add the #student-wins channel to my Role Project.

2026-04-14 — Used Student Reply on a module 3 question. Voice was off, too formal. Adding 3 more casual examples to section 6 this weekend.

2026-04-15 — Used Delegation-to-Skill to turn my weekly payment-reminder task into a skill. Worked first try. Sending to Albert for review.

2026-04-16 — Tried Airtable Query for the at-risk student list. Took 3 tries to get the phrasing right. Next time I'll start with "find records where…" instead of "show me…".
```

---

## The weekly refinement (10 minutes, Friday afternoon)

Read back your entries from the week. Answer three questions:

1. **What skill did I use most?** That skill is carrying its weight — keep it sharp.
2. **What skill did I not use at all?** Either the trigger is wrong, the prompt is wrong, or the skill is not actually needed. Fix or retire.
3. **What did I wish Claude could do that it didn't?** That is a candidate for the Delegation-to-Skill meta-skill. Build it next week.

Update your Role Project (add new facts, fix voice examples), edit or retire skills, and push any generated skills back to Albert for the canonical repo.

---

## Where to keep the log

Simplest: a single Notion page in your personal workspace titled "My Wins Log." New entries append to the top. No structure beyond date and line.

If you prefer a text file, use Apple Notes, Obsidian, or whatever you already trust. The format does not matter; the habit does.

---

## Accountability for the first 4 weeks

Share your daily line in the internal team Slack channel (or whatever channel Albert designates for this) for the first 4 weeks. After that, it can go personal. The public sharing period exists to build the habit and surface patterns across the team — you will notice what your teammates are using and it will shape your own library.

---

## What not to do

- **Do not skip days.** One missed day becomes two, and the log dies. If you forget, catch up the next morning.
- **Do not turn it into a journal.** One line. Detail goes in the Friday reflection or in your Role Project updates.
- **Do not lie to look good.** "Didn't use any skill today, fell back to manual work" is a valid entry and a useful signal.

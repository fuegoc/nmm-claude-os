# My Voice Samples — [Your Name]

This is your personal voice file. It tells the Student Reply and Student Feedback skills how *you* write, not just how the team writes. The skills will prefer this file over the team baseline when you have it in your Project Knowledge.

**When to fill this out:** after you have been using Claude OS for a week or two and you notice the drafts feel "too Albert" for your style. That is the signal that you are ready to add your personal voice layer. Until then, you can run on the team baseline alone.

**How to use this template:**

1. Replace `[Your Name]` in the title above with your actual first name.
2. Paste 5 to 10 real messages you have written into the slots below. Mix contexts: a student reply answering a common question, a student reply handling a hard case, a Slack message to the team, a feedback note on student work, a "no that's out of scope" response. Real messages from the last 30 days work best.
3. Fill in the voice rules section at the bottom with your own preferences.
4. Save the file as `voice-samples-[your-first-name].md` (e.g. `voice-samples-jesse.md`) and upload it to your Claude Desktop Project's Knowledge area, alongside the team baseline file.
5. The Student Reply and Student Feedback skills will automatically prefer your personal file from that point forward.

**Tip for editing:** if you keep your messages in Notion (recommended), duplicate the "Voice Samples Template" sub-page from the No More Mondays Claude OS handbook into your own workspace, paste your messages into the Notion page, then click the `···` menu on the page → Export → Markdown → upload the exported file to Claude Desktop. No text editor needed.

## How the skills use this file

The Student Reply and Student Feedback skills look for files in your Project Knowledge in this order of preference:

1. **Your personal voice file** (`voice-samples-[your-name].md`). If present, this is the primary reference for tone, structure, and vocabulary.
2. **The team baseline** (`voice-samples-nmm-team.md`). Always present. Used as a sanity check and as the source of NMM house rules.
3. **Section 6 of your Role Project** (in Project Instructions). Structural voice rules that apply across everything.
4. **The repo fallback** (`handbook/house-voice-nmm.md` in the GitHub repo). Last-resort fallback if nothing else is available.

So uploading this personal file moves you from "drafts in the team voice" to "drafts in your voice with the team voice as the safety net." That is the upgrade.
